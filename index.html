🐦 Junco Survivor Game

Survive environmental challenges, reproduce, pass on inherited boldness alleles, and track CORT stress hormone levels.

Junco Survivor: Student Instructions

Your mission: Use the game data to explain why city-dwelling juncos may become bolder than mountain-dwelling juncos over generations.

1. Get into a team

Work in a group of 2–4 students. Your group will focus on either Urban UCSD juncos or Mountain Mt. Laguna juncos.

2. Start and play

  1. Choose the number of generations and starting population.
  2. Click Start Game.
  3. Read each environmental event.
  4. Choose a strategy and click Run Next Generation.
  5. Read the outcome cards and record your data.

3. Make sense of the evidence

  • Track total birds, percent bold, boldness allele frequency, and CORT.
  • Run the Common Garden Experiment after at least 2 generations.
  • Graph one trend and explain how selection changed the population.

🧪 Common Garden Experiment

Scientists tested whether boldness was inherited or learned by raising UCSD and Mt. Laguna juncos in the same controlled environment.

Controlled Variables

  • same aviary conditions
  • same food access
  • same human exposure
  • same nesting environment

Question

If both groups are raised in the same environment, will UCSD birds still behave differently from Mt. Laguna birds?

Run at least 2 generations, then click Run Common Garden Experiment.
